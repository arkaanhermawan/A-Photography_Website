About

Photography comes from the Greek words "photos" for light and "grapho" for painting. Therefore, the concept of photography is the process of painting using the medium of light. The photo process uses a method that utilizes the reflection of light and hits the subject with a light-sensitive device called a camera. Photography became popular in the early 19th century, in 1839. France officially declared photography as a technological development. In the 5th century Before The Common Era a man named Mo Ti saw a phenomenon called pinhole, which means that when there is a small hole in the wall of a dark room, it reflects an image. The camera obscura phenomenon was first discovered by Mo Ti. Centuries later, Aristotle and an Arab scholar named Ibnu Al Haitam tried to invent and develop the device we know today as a camera. In 1558, Italian scientists invented the camera obscura to help painters take photographs. Then, in 1611, Johannes Kepler created a portable tent-shaped camera model. It was then developed into a camera that is widely used today.
